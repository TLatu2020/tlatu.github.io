Keeping Fish Haven

There is no surprise that this particular area of Idaho is well known for its fish, hence the name "Fish Haven". When two gentlemen, by the name of Joseph C. Rich and John Bagley, were out surveying the a lake in the local area, they threw a seine in the mouth of a river, and from that seine, were able to catch 96 large trout. This is where the name of the City Fish Haven comes from, and we would like to keep it that way.

Inorder to keep it that way, the local government seeks to preserve and sustain the wildlife of the area, to avoid over fishing and destroying what makes Fish Haven, Fish Haven. To accomplish this, seasons and rules have been set forth, that determine when fishing season for certain areas are permissible and when the fishing season ends to allow the areas to rest and recuperate. Rules are also set forth, depending on the lakes and areas you go fishing, about what type of fish you are allowed to catch and how many you are allowed to catch. It is also important to remember that along with bag limits, how many you can catch, size limits may also apply, to ensure that young fish are allowed to survive and help grow it's population.

Just as much as the local government plays an important role in trying to preserve and sustain the wildlife, each of us also plays an important role as well, by getting to know the seasons and rules before we go out and fish. For information about the current seasons and rules of the local areas you can go to idfg.idaho.gov.
